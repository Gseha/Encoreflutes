What are Flute scales? Which is best?


Till now we came across various instances about various scales of flute like sometimes I used the C scale flute and sometimes the C sharp.


But what exactly is a flute scale and why different flutes can have different scales?


We know that the notes of the Inidan gamut(sven notes of music) are known as Sargam.Just as English word "alphabet" is derived from greek letters 'alpha, beta', in a similar way the words 'Sargam' is derived from 'Sa-Re-Ga-Ma'. Sa Re Ga Ma are simply the initial notes of the Indian classical music. Thes swaras are SA, RE, GA, MA, PA, DHA, NI and ŚA. The intervals of the Indian scale are essentially the same as those of the western scale. The notes in the Western scale are evenly spaced; the ones in the eastern scale follow the natural divisions of vibrational frequencies.

The Swar Sa and Pa do not alter their positions hence are called Achal Swars. They are used as a bookmark to identify the scale. The position of Sa and Pa determines the Scale of the song. Thses notes donot have a variant.


Rest of the notes do have a variant and hence their position alters, due to which they are called Chal Swars. Re, Ga, Dha and Ni have a Komal variant whereas Ma has a teevra variant. We will study about this later in the Komal and Teevra SwarsKomal and Teevra Swars section.




Fixing of Kharaj Note and deterrmination of the flute scale


Kharaj refers to Fixed or Constant. Sa and Pa are the Kharaj Swars.
Note the Swars S R G m P D N Ś are the shuddh swars which we have used till now while the swars r g M d n are Komal and Teevra Swars which we will deal later.



We can fix any Kharaj note that is "Sa" and correspoding attached notes of the scale will be accoding to the given arrangement- S r R g G m M Pd D n N Ś .


Here in the below picture we selected the first black key or C# Key as our Kharaj Sa.
Hence Re is D#, Ga is F, ma is F#, Pa is G#, Dha is A#, Ni is C and Śa is C#.


Since Sa is C# Key. The scale of the sargam is C#.

Each time we change our Kharaj note Sa, the position of notes in a scale will also be changed. Let us take our Kharaj note Sa as First whit key that is let Sa be C note. Here is the Sargam when Sa is C.
Therefore, Re is D, Ga is E, ma is F, Pa is G, Dha is A, Ni is B and Śa is C.


Sargam when Kharaj Note Sa is C.

Similarly when Sa of my flute is note C, this means the scale of my flute is C, if Sa is D, then the scale will be D. Then my correspoding notes will change according to the position of Sa. Re in a C scale flute will be D where as Re in a D scale flute would be E.

Which Scale is the best?


All are the best. All of them are capable of producing sounds of Sa Re Ga Ma, So songs can be played on all of them.


We use different scale flutes for different songs with accordance to the song pitch and the softness or shrillness of the songs. We will be learning more about it later in the journey.

Till then Happy Fluting!!
